Motivation

Abstract

Vision-and-Language Navigation (VLN) has made progress on long-horizon, multi-stage missions. Yet prevailing formulations remain single-agentâ€“centric, which constrains multi-robot execution by limiting parallelism, ignoring inter-robot interference, and overlooking time and energy efficiency.

To address these limitations, we propose a new benchmark, termed Collaborative Long-Horizon VLN (CLH-VLN), targeting task-to-team decomposition and efficiency-aware role allocation for collaborative robots. To support CLH-VLN at scale, we introduce NavCraft, an automated, graph-grounded platform. It implements a two-stage hierarchical LLM agent. NavCraft-S proposes a long-horizon base mission for the primary robot. NavCraft-C then instantiates helper robots, subgoals, and handoffs tailored to that mission. The agents operate scene-graph-in-the-loop over a semantics-and-geometry-aware scene graph derived from Habitat-Sim. This enables reachability checks, travel-time and energy estimation, and interference assessment with iterative plan repair. An on-graph efficiency skill library then helps agents optimize team size, subgoal assignment, and rendezvous scheduling in a closed loop.

To accurately evaluate complex tasks, we release Collaborative Long-Horizon Planning and Reasoning for VLN (CLHPR-VLN), a benchmark of 4,048 single and collaborative episodes with a Collaboration-Type (CT) taxonomy for controlled analysis. To our knowledge, it is the first benchmark specifically designed for CLH-VLN. CLHPR-VLN achieves 20\% average efficiency improvement and higher success rates over single-robot-based VLN.
